The War That Made Putin

Jun 20, 2026 · 16 min · 31 sources

Moscow. Autumn 1999.

It's the autumn of 1999. In the space of two weeks, bombs tear through apartment blocks in three Russian cities while people sleep. Around 300 are dead. The country is terrified, and it wants someone to blame.

In front of the cameras stands a man almost nobody had heard of a month earlier. A grey former spy named Vladimir Putin, made prime minister in August by a fading Boris Yeltsin. He promises to hunt the killers down without mercy. Then he sends the army back into Chechnya, the small mountain republic that had humiliated that same army three years before.

Eighteen months later, Putin runs the country. He has run it ever since.

This is the story of the war that put him there.

The Second Chechen War.
