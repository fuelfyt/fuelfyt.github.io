The Last Time Russia Lost a War

Jun 14, 2026 · 16 min · 19 sources

Grozny, Chechnya. December 31st, 1994.

It's the dead of winter, and the city is on fire. An artillery barrage has set the oil tanks alight in the early morning, and black smoke is rolling across the rooftops. Russian tanks are pushing into the streets from four directions. Inside them, thousands of young conscripts are riding toward a battle their commanders swore would never come. And up in the apartment blocks above them, Chechen fighters are watching the columns roll in, holding their fire.

The soldiers think this will be easy. They've been told the enemy is untrained and disorganized, that the sight of armor in the streets will be enough to make them give up. They are about to find out how wrong that is.

This is the story of the First Chechen War.
